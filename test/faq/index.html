OK, so what can I do about all this?

The reality (and tragedy) of the situation is that it's almost certainly too late to prevent climate change from irreversibly damaging and degrading our world. If you're not convinced of that, read Deep Adaptation: A Map for Navigating Climate Tragedy. Helpfully, that paper outlines four kinds of responses we can make:

  1. Resilience asks us “how do we keep what we really want to keep?”
  2. Relinquishment asks us “what do we need to let go of in order to not make matters worse?”
  3. Restoration asks us “what can we bring back to help us with the coming difficulties and tragedies?”
  4. Reconciliation asks “with what and whom can we make peace as we face our mutual mortality?”

What your response looks like is likely to be different to mine, and to other people's. For example, I'm spending a lot of time thinking about 'restoration' at the moment, asking myself what knowledge, technologies, and habits of mind we might be able to bring back to make life more sustainable (and enjoyable!)
